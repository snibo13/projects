# H, T, I, M and Other letters

There's always the archetype of the T shaped person. Someone who is an expert with deep focus in one field while being somewhat proficient in others (Depending on your typeface this varies). I think this is a wonderful idea but I tried to extend it a little further. 

Imagine going through the alphabet and considering each letter as a representative of both depth and breadth of knowledge. Some don't really make sens (g, j, l, z) but others do serve as interesting models for how one could pursue knowledge.

### A
I kept this on here just to bookend, but I'll be honest. A doesn't really make sense as a model to me.

### E
Lots of breadth at different levels. Understand one thing deeply and the others at a more surface level but this continues to adjacent topics at various levels of depth

... F is kinda like E

### H
H is interesting. Two deep pillars with an interconnected bridge. This could be adjacent, or unrelated fields, or it could be breadth with two pillars seperated by a span. 

### I
Breadth at the top, breadth at the bottom. 

### L
Only breadth at the bottom, kindof an expert in one field or niche within a larger wrapper. Imagine someone who can play string instruments like the violin, cello and bass but is atrocious on the keys

### M

Two pillars and breadth leading to a connection (See W)

### V
Two disjoint pillars that eventually become the same topic
Imagine if you drilled really far down into mathematics of physics, eventually they become governed by the same things and their limitations are one and the same

### W
I image W as three fields that all share some connections. For example, music. Understanding music theory will help you play both the drums and guitar but all of those sets of knowledge are kinda their own thing.

### X
Like a V but after meeting the two paths diverge again

### Y
Two things that connect and become a focus. Like math and psychology meeting to become a love of poker

### Z
Basically the same as I, if you treat left to right as some sort of time axis then maybe the breadth leads to depth which then expands to more breadth. This is honestly pretty consistent with learning anything. You learn something go in depth a bit realise how little you know, learn some breadth and recurse.


# An architectural aside

In the time between me starting this blog and posting it, I have been understandably been thinking quite a lot about my own model and what I want it to be. In part because I have been on a bit of an architecture kick as of late, I found the best model lay outside of the alphabet.

The Parthenon

Fittingly, created as a temple to the Greek goddess of Wisdom Athena, the Parthenon has stood since the 5th century BC. It's layout is called peripteral which describes a building type where a central room (A Naos of Cella) is sandwiched between two pronaos, kind of antechambers (The Parthenon only has one but I don't think that matters?). That's fine and all but the important part to my model is the Pteron. the Pteron is a surrounding colunnade around the structure. When you look at photos of the Parthenon that is largely what remains standing.
